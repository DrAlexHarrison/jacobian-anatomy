## 1. The map and the basic certificates

F : C³ → C³,

```
F₁ = (1+xy)³ z + y²(1+xy)(4+3xy)
F₂ = y + 3x(1+xy)² z + 3xy²(4+3xy)
F₃ = 2x − 3x²y − x³z
```

det JF ≡ −2 (three independent derivations: sympy symbolic;
a hand-rolled exact-fraction polynomial engine with no CAS dependency;
numeric finite differences as sanity check). `verify/01_basic.py`.

F(0,0,−1/4) = F(1,−3/2,13/2) = F(−1,3/2,13/2) = (−1/4,0,0), three
distinct points. Hence F is a Keller map that is not injective and the
Jacobian Conjecture (statement and history, arXiv 2512.23614) is false
in dimension 3, hence in every dimension ≥ 3 by padding with identity
coordinates. Among the problem's long entanglements: Yitang Zhang's
doctoral thesis was devoted to this very conjecture (Y. Zhang, *The
Jacobian Conjecture and the Degree of Field Extension*, Ph.D. thesis,
Purdue University, Dec. 1991 (advisor T. T. Moh);
docs.lib.purdue.edu/dissertations/AAI9215703). Direct computation shows that fiber is complete: the ideal has quotient
dimension exactly 3. `verify/01_basic.py`.

**Formal verification.** The first public Lean formalization, as far as we
can tell, is **deancureton/jacobian**, which proves the stronger all-characteristics
statement `not_jacobianConjecture_all_char`, including characteristic 2 via
the rescaled map G = diag(1/2,1/2,−1/2)∘F∘diag(1,2,2) with det ≡ 1, which
identifies (0,1,0) and (1,1,0) in char 2 (a trick we record here with credit).
Our `JC.lean` is an **independent second formalization** (map, Jacobian
determinant via mathlib `pderiv` + `Matrix.det`, non-injectivity over ℚ;
axioms: `propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound` only), written without
knowledge of theirs. We also ran a full build-and-axiom audit of their
repository (`audit/deancureton-audit.md`): verdict **clean**, `lake build`
succeeds on the pinned toolchain, no `sorry`/`native_decide`/custom axioms,
every theorem depends on exactly the three standard axioms, and the encoding
is faithful. (One caveat recorded there: for char p > 0 the conjecture
was already classically false, so the all-characteristics breadth is formally,
not mathematically, novel.)

## 2. The C\*-symmetry

F is equivariant for the C\*-actions
λ_t(x,y,z) = (tx, t⁻¹y, t⁻²z) on the source and
τ_t(a,b,c) = (t⁻²a, t⁻¹b, tc) on the target:

 F ∘ λ_t = τ_t ∘ F (weights (1,−1,−2) → (−2,−1,1)).

The weight vector is **forced**: the weight lattice computed from the
monomial supports has rank exactly one, with generator (1,−1,−2) → (−2,−1,1),
so this C\* is the *entire* torus symmetry of F, not a slice of a bigger
one (independently re-verified: 35/35 exact checks,
`audit/equivariance-review.md`). The visible mirror symmetry of the announced
fiber
(two points swapped by (x,y,z) ↦ (−x,−y,z)) is the t = −1 element of this
action. Every object below (the branch quartic D, the invariant
27ac²−9bc+8, the missed curve Γ) is invariant, and the entire anatomy is
τ-equivariant. the one-parameter family of triple-collision
fibers observed on X by Siphon (@NinzaK40245), verbatim
F(0,0,−1/(4r²)) = F(r,−3/(2r),13/(2r²)) = F(−r,3/(2r),13/(2r²)) for all
r ∈ C\*, is exactly the λ_r-orbit of the announced fiber, with images the
punctured a-axis (−1/(4r²), 0, 0): he found the orbit without naming the
group. (Three-point fibers are in fact *generic*, §4; this family is
distinguished as the orbit of the announced fiber, with wholly rational
witnesses, over the fixed axis of the induced mirror.)
`verify/03_new_facts.py`, `verify/05_presentations_rigidity.py`.

## 3. The covering cubic and the lifting law

Let r = x/(1+xy), a coordinate first proposed in the X thread by Ted Punt,
whose observation we verify and complete here.

2F₁r³ − F₂r² + 2r − F₃ ≡ 0. Thus every fiber point of
q = (a,b,c) yields a root of the **covering cubic**

 C_q(r) = 2a·r³ − b·r² + 2r − c,

which is τ-equivariant of weight 1 and startlingly simple: the counterexample
is, fiberwise, the universal family of these cubics. `verify/03_new_facts.py`.

**(three presentations, one covering)** The other algebraic
presentation found on X (slowform333's v = y + 1/x with
c·v³ − 2v² + b·v − 2a = 0) satisfies v = (1+xy)/x = **1/r**: his cubic is
exactly the coefficient-reversed (reciprocal-root) form of C_q. And x itself
is a third primitive element (§5), so by a general fact about cubic
extensions, any two primitive elements are related by a Möbius
transformation over the base field (a bilinear relation has 4 coefficients,
vanishing on the 3 conjugate pairs imposes 3 conditions; the solution is
unique up to scale, hence Galois-stable, hence defined over the base), all
three presentations are Möbius-equivalent forms of one covering. Explicit
pushforward certificate: Res_r(C_q, x·(br² − 4r + 3c) − 2r²) = 4c·(x-cubic).
`verify/05_presentations_rigidity.py`, `verify/06_identities.py`.

**(rigidity)** In the natural ansatz replacing 4+3xy by q = t + s·xy
throughout, requiring det J to be constant forces **(t,s) = (4,3)** uniquely
(ten nonconstant coefficients of det J vanish simultaneously only there,
where det = −2). The counterexample is an isolated point of its own family:
this map is not one of a cheap continuum. (Three senses of "family" are in
play and should not be conflated: this coefficient ansatz; the C\*-action of
§2, which fixes F while permuting fibers; and the Aut(C³)-composition orbit
of §6, which is trivially infinite.) `verify/05_presentations_rigidity.py`.

**(section)** Conversely, roots lift by explicit rational formulas:

 y(r) = −(b r² + 3c − 6r)/(2r²), x(r) = r/(1 − r·y(r)),
 z(r) = (2x − 3x²y − c)/x³,

and in fact F₂ = b and F₃ = c hold *exactly* on the section: the covering
cubic C_q(r) = 0 is precisely the one remaining condition F₁ = a.
`verify/04_parametrization.py`, `audit/equivariance-review.md`.

**(wall identity)** As an exact cofactor identity,

 (1 − r·y(r)) − C_q′(r)/2 = −3·C_q(r)/(2r),

so on the cubic, 1 − r·y(r) = C_q′(r)/2: the section degenerates (x → ∞)
exactly at multiple roots. **A root of C_q lifts to an affine preimage iff it
is a simple root.** `verify/04_parametrization.py`.

**(lifting law)** Homogenize: C̃_q(r:s) = 2a·r³ − b·r²s + 2rs² − cs³
on P¹. Then for *every* q ∈ C³,

 #F⁻¹(q) = #{simple roots of C̃_q in P¹}.

The point at infinity (1:0) is a root iff a = 0, is simple iff moreover
b ≠ 0, and its sheet is the surface {1+xy = 0}, mapping onto {a=0, b≠0} via
(x, −1/x, z) ↦ (0, 2/x, 5x − x³z); the root r = 0 occurs iff c = 0 and is
always simple, its sheet being the x = 0 stratum on which F restricts to the
isomorphism (0,y,z) ↦ (z+4y², y, 0). In affine terms: for a ≠ 0 the count is
#{simple roots of C_q}; for a = 0 it is #{simple roots of the degree ≤ 2
remainder} + [b ≠ 0]. (Stated affinely without that clause the law fails
exactly on {a = 0, b ≠ 0}; the projective form is the correct one.)
Injectivity of the fiber → roots correspondence is the identity y ≡ y(r) at
b = F₂, c = F₃, r = x/(1+xy), which holds identically on the source; with the
section, the wall identity, and the two boundary charts this assembles into a
complete proof at every point, written out in `audit/equivariance-review.md`
(addendum), independently certified 23/23 plus a 14-point stratum-spanning
battery. `verify/04_parametrization.py`, certificates 1–8 (certificate 8 is
the uniqueness forcing and the source identity 1 − r·y = 1/(1+xy), which
together close at-most-one-per-root and no-lift-at-multiple-roots).

## 4. The stratification and the exact image

Write D = 27a²c² − 18abc + 16a + b³c − b² and E = 27ac² − 9bc + 8. The spine
of everything below is a pair of **crown-jewel identities**, holding in
Q[x,y,z] by pure expansion, no elimination theory, no genericity caveats:

 I_x : D(F)·x³ + (4 − 3F₂F₃)·x − 2F₃ ≡ 0
 I_y : 2y³ − 3F₂·y² + 18F₁·y + (27F₁²F₃ − 18F₁F₂ + F₂³) ≡ 0

both (`verify/06_identities.py`). So the coordinates of any
preimage of (a,b,c) satisfy the x-cubic D·x³ + (4−3bc)x − 2c and the y-cubic
with *constant* leading coefficient 2, making "y never escapes to infinity"
a theorem, not an observation. Exact constants: disc_r C_q = −4·D,
disc of the x-cubic = −4·E²·D, and Res_y(G₁,G₂) = −c·x⁹·(the x-cubic).
By direct computation, D is squarefree and irreducible over ℚ (gcd with its gradient
is 1). In the r-coordinate the branch locus is exactly the quartic V(D); the
locus E = 0 ∖ V(D) is where two sheets merely share an x-coordinate, an
artifact of projecting to x, not fiber degeneration, since étaleness forbids
actual ramification. the exact relation

 (4 − 3bc)³ + 27c²·D = E²,

which pins the missed curve as an intersection of two invariant
hypersurfaces: **Γ = V(D) ∩ V(4 − 3bc)** exactly (`verify/06_identities.py`).

The lifting law then gives the full stratification (a cubic has 3 simple
roots, or a double + a simple, or a triple root):

- **off V(D)**: 3 simple roots (projective, per the lifting law's a = 0
 clause) → **3 preimages**. F is étale (Keller) and generically 3-to-1;
- **on V(D) ∖ Γ**: double root (walled, escapes) + simple root → **1
 preimage**;
- **on Γ**: for q = Γ(t) = (4/(27t²), 4/(3t), t), the cubic is a
 perfect cube, C = (8/(27t²))(r − 3t/2)³, all three sheets merge and escape
 simultaneously → **0 preimages**. Conversely the triple-root locus of the
 family (match coefficients: b = 6aρ, 2 = 6aρ², c = 2aρ³) is exactly Γ,
 with prime defining ideal (bc − 4/3, 12a − b²) (coordinate ring C[t, t⁻¹]),
 a closed single orbit with trivial stabilizer.

**Image.** F(C³) = C³ ∖ Γ, both halves:

*Γ is missed, in one line*: if F(p) = Γ(t), then since D and 4 − 3bc vanish
identically on Γ, identity I_x evaluated at p reads 0·x³ + 0·x − 2t = 0,
forcing t = 0: contradiction. (Independent second proof: the ideal
⟨F₁ − 4/27, F₂ − 4/3, F₃ − 1⟩ has Groebner basis [1], a Nullstellensatz unit
certificate, empty over C, not merely without rational points, and
equivariance transports emptiness along the whole orbit;
`audit/equivariance-review.md`.)

*Everything else is hit, constructively*: {c = 0} is covered by the
explicit polynomial section F(0, b, a − 4b²) = (a, b, 0). For c ≠ 0 with
(a,b,c) ∉ Γ, the x-cubic P(x) = Dx³ + (4−3bc)x − 2c is nonconstant (D and
4−3bc cannot both vanish off Γ when c ≠ 0, by the E² identity); pick a root
x₀, automatically nonzero since P(0) = −2c ≠ 0. Eliminating z through F₃
(legitimate: x₀ ≠ 0) gives G₁, G₂ in (x,y) with y-leading coefficients −cx³
and −3cx², both nonvanishing at x₀; since Res_y(G₁,G₂) = −c·x⁹·P vanishes at
x₀ with surviving leading coefficients, the specializations share a root y₀
(resultant specialization, Cox–Little–O'Shea, *Ideals, Varieties, and
Algorithms*, Ch. 3 §6), and z₀ = (2x₀ − 3x₀²y₀ − c)/x₀³ completes a preimage.
(Alternative proof via the lifting law: off Γ the covering cubic always has
a simple projective root.) So the counterexample fails injectivity and
surjectivity at once, in the most economical way the symmetry allows.
Cross-checks at rational sample points (3/1/0 fiber dimensions, single
preimage (1/2, −8/3, 16) over (−16/27, 0, 1)): `verify/02_anatomy.py`,
`verify/04_parametrization.py`, `verify/06_identities.py`.

**Non-properness.** V(D) is exactly the Jelonek non-properness set (Jelonek, Ann. Polon. Math. 58 (1993)): F is non-proper over V(D) (sheets
escape, the walled roots), and proper over the complement: if some q ∉ V(D) had an
escape sequence p_k → ∞ with F(p_k) → q, take disjoint balls around q's
three preimages; étaleness makes their images cover a neighborhood N of q,
so for large k the point F(p_k) ∈ N would have one preimage in each ball
*plus* p_k outside all three, a four-point fiber, contradicting the global
bound of 3. So no escape occurs over C³ ∖ V(D): F is proper there. explicit escape, as observed by
@Mechborne in the announcement thread (correctly *explaining* the map's
consistency with Hadamard's criterion, not, as some summaries mislabeled it,
an objection), sharpened and verified here: along t ↦ (t, −1/t, 5/t²) one has
1+xy ≡ 0 identically, so F = (0, 2/t, 0) *exactly* → (0,0,0) ∈ V(D) while the
arc itself escapes. (The origin is nonetheless *in* the image: it is its own
unique preimage, consistent with the one-point fibers on V(D) ∖ Γ.) `verify/03_new_facts.py`. Non-properness is forced: a
**connected** finite étale cover of the simply connected C³ would be trivial,
so a proper (hence finite étale of degree 3) Keller self-map cannot exist.

**y is proper.** by identity I_y: the y-coordinate of any
preimage satisfies a cubic with constant leading coefficient 2, so y is
integral over C[a,b,c] and never escapes; all non-properness lives in x and
z jointly. `verify/02_anatomy.py`, `verify/06_identities.py`.

## 5. Monodromy S₃

over C(a,b,c), no sample points: (i) the covering cubic
C_q(r) = 2a·r³ − b·r² + 2r − c is irreducible over C(a,b,c): it is linear
in c with unit c-coefficient, so in any factorization over C[a,b,c][r] the
c-free factor must divide a unit (match c-coefficients), the content in r
is 1, and Gauss's lemma finishes (`verify/06_identities.py`, cert 9).
(ii) r is a primitive element outright: the §3 section formulas express
x, y, z as rational functions of (r; a, b, c) identically on the source
(certificate 8), so C(a,b,c)(r) = C(x,y,z); with (i),
[C(x,y,z) : C(a,b,c)] = 3, which also upgrades "F is generically 3-to-1"
from computation to theorem. (iii) disc_r C_q = −4D, and −4D is a
non-square in C(a,b,c) since D is squarefree, so the Galois group of the
closure is transitive, not contained in A₃, hence **S₃**. The x- and
v-presentations inherit everything through the Möbius equivalences of §3. In hindsight
this was forced: A₃ would make the extension Galois, and a Galois Keller map
is an automorphism, equivalence (g)⇔(a) of (Bass–Connell–Wright,
Bull. AMS 7 (1982), Thm 2.1; the implication (g)⇒(a) is due to Campbell,
Math. Ann. 205 (1973) 243–248, with algebraic proofs by Razar, Wright, and
Oda). A generic-degree-3 counterexample has no choice but full monodromy. An
independent confirmation by direct computation, via specialization: at (a,b,c) = (3/7, −2/5, 1/3) the cubic is
irreducible over ℚ with non-square discriminant, so the specialized group is
S₃, embedding into the generic *arithmetic* Galois group (over ℚ(a,b,c));
the geometric statement over C(a,b,c) rests on the discriminant argument
above. `verify/03_new_facts.py`.

## 6. F is prime

modulo standard theorems: if F = G∘H with polynomial self-maps of
C³, the chain rule gives (det JG ∘ H)·det JH = −2, so det JH is a unit in
C[x,y,z], i.e. a nonzero constant; and H is dominant (F is), so det JG ∘ H
constant forces det JG constant too: both factors are Keller. Generic
degrees multiply and 3 is prime, so one factor is birational and Keller,
hence an automorphism by Keller's own classical theorem, a Keller map with a
rational inverse has a polynomial inverse (Keller 1939). (Alternative
chain: étale + birational ⇒ open immersion by Zariski's Main Theorem ⇒
injective ⇒ surjective by Ax–Grothendieck ⇒ automorphism.) So the
counterexample does not decompose through any simpler non-invertible map.
Composition with automorphisms, by contrast, trivially yields infinitely many
counterexamples (the announcement thread's explainer exhibits composites with
component degrees 14/20/7): the real classification problem is up to
Aut(C³)-composition, and primality says this map is a minimal representative
of its class.

## 7. Consequences for the conjecture landscape

- **Minimality of the counterexample within its symmetry class** (v1.2): no C*-equivariant Keller map of total degree
 ≤ 6 in C³ (canonical matched weights) fails injectivity. The mechanism
 needs degree 7. Complementary and independent: Shaska (arXiv 2607.20210)
 proves positive-weight equivariant Keller maps are automorphisms in every
 dimension, and all signatures in dimension two, at unbounded degree, and
 proposes the classification-by-signature program of which the degree ≤ 6,
 dimension-3 case is carried out in `classification/`. Scope and evidence: `classification/CLASSIFICATION.md`
 (371 isomorphism classes: 365 by direct Gröbner certificates, 6 by
 hand theorems; zero non-unit collision certificates anywhere;
 the non-equivariant degree ≤ 6 question remains open).

- **Jacobian Conjecture**: false for n ≥ 3. The case n = 2 remains open; the
 sharpest published exclusion (Guccione–Guccione–Horruitiner–Valqui,
 arXiv 2204.14178) rules out all planar counterexamples of degree below 125
 *except* the degree pairs (72,108)/(108,72): the planar frontier sits
 exactly there.
- **Dixmier Conjecture**: DC_n ⇒ JC_n (van den Essen, *Polynomial
 Automorphisms and the Jacobian Conjecture*, Thm 10.4.2), so **DC_n is false
 for all n ≥ 3**. Explicitly: since det JF is constant, A = (JF)⁻¹ has
 polynomial entries, and the derivations D_j = Σ_k A_{kj}∂_k
 pairwise commute and satisfy [D_j, F_i] = δ_ij (`verify/01_basic.py`; the
 construction is classical, Wright, and independently P. Nousiainen, as
 presented in (BCW, Bull. AMS 7 (1982), §2)), so
 x_i ↦ F_i, ∂_j ↦ D_j defines an endomorphism of the third Weyl algebra A₃
 which, by the theorem above, is not an automorphism. The ladder now reads:
 for n = 1 a proof has been *claimed* by Zheglov (arXiv 2410.06959,
 under review since 2024; v5 Jan 2026), if it stands, the Dixmier family
 splits: **true for n = 1, open for n = 2, false for n ≥ 3**. For DC₂ one
 open transfer leg each way remains: DC₂ ⇒ JC₂ and JC₂ ⇒ DC₁; the
 classical JC₄ ⇒ DC₂ leg of the Tsuchimoto / Belov-Kanel–Kontsevich stable
 equivalence is now vacuous. Alpöge has publicly concurred with the
 Dixmier/Poisson consequence in the announcement thread.
- **Poisson conjecture**: false at the family level via the equivalence of the
 Dixmier, Jacobian and Poisson conjectures (Adjamagbo–van den Essen,
 Acta Math. Vietnam. 32 (2007)).
- **Mathieu conjecture**: false (it implies JC (Mathieu 1997)). The
 abelian case is the Duistermaat–van der Kallen *theorem* (1998) and stands;
 the failure now has a named family of addresses, with one point of care.
 Zwart's Thm 2.2, "Assume the Mathieu Conjecture for SU(N); then the
 Jacobian Conjecture on C^N is true" (arXiv 2511.16561), is stated
 per-N for the full JC, but its proof (Thm 4.16 + Cor 4.24 there) supports
 the *homogeneous-form* JC on C^N; and homogeneous-form JC in dimension 3
 is TRUE (de Bondt–van den Essen, J. Algebra 294 (2005), cited
 above), so no three-variable map can falsify MC(SU(3)) through this chain.
 What does follow: the map lifts (Bass–Connell–Wright) to a
 homogeneous-form counterexample in explicit dimension **M = 79**
 (`druzkowski/`, independently verified four times), so **the Mathieu
 conjecture is false for SU(N) for all N ≥ 79** (for N > 79, pad the C⁷⁹
 lift by identity coordinates: the padded map is still cubic-homogeneous,
 Keller, and non-injective, and the chain reruns verbatim; see
 `audit/su79-review.md` §1 step 5). The minimal such N is a
 new open question: N_min ∈ [4, 79] in general (dimension 3 is excluded
 for homogeneous maps of *all* degrees by de Bondt–van den Essen 2005),
 and ∈ [5, 79] for the cubic route used here (Hubbers 1994 settles the
 dim-4 cubic case); 79 is an upper bound of the plain reduction, not an
 optimum. SU(2) remains unresolved (via this chain it governs only
 homogeneous-form JC₂, which is classically true; proving Mathieu for
 SU(2) is an active program (Müger–Tuset, arXiv 2210.06582)).
 **v1.1:** the SU(79) statement is now a
 machine-certified theorem. `mathieu-su79/` carries the runnable
 certificate: L = id + H on C⁷⁹ cubic-homogeneous Keller with an explicit
 3-point collision, whose formal inverse is provably non-polynomial with
 explicit nonzero homogeneous components at degrees 3–11; the review
 report and an independent from-scratch verifier (term-by-term agreement,
 disjoint algorithms) are `audit/su79-review.md` and
 `audit/su79_crosscheck.py`. The bridge is cited Mathieu-primary
 (Prop 2.2(ii) + Cor 1.3/1.7): review found a proof gap at
 the fixed-ξ step of Zwart's Thm 4.23 (the theorem survives via Mathieu's
 Baire-generic ξ; details in the report). Consequence: a violating
 finite-type pair (f, g) on SU(79) **exists**; writing one explicitly
 requires Mathieu's Cartan twist by a generic ξ (open work). The per-N
 scope reading above is additionally confirmed from Mathieu's original
 paper by two independent full-text reads: Prop 5.3's proof is
 homogeneous-form-only, and the only homogeneous-to-general bridge is
 Bass–Connell–Wright, which inflates dimension.
- **Zhao's vanishing conjecture**: false *as a family* (the equivalence with
 JC is family-to-family, and the JC ⇒ VC direction consumes JC in dimension
 n+1 (Zhao, Trans. AMS 359 (2007), Thm 7.2 and Prop. 7.4)); Zhao's
 image conjecture: false (it implies JC). Any explicit degree-4
 Hessian-nilpotent witness necessarily has ≥ 6 variables and genuinely
 complex coefficients (VC is proven for n ≤ 4, and real Hessian-nilpotent
 polynomials are trivial); construction from this map is in progress.
- **Reductions run backwards**: by Bass–Connell–Wright (Bull. AMS 7
 (1982)) and Drużkowski (Math. Ann. 264 (1983)), the failure
 propagates to their normal forms, and we have carried it out.
 By direct computation, this map lifts explicitly to a cubic-homogeneous
 counterexample x + H on **C⁷⁹** (det ≡ 1, JH nilpotent) and to a
 Drużkowski cubic-linear one X − (AX)^∗³ on **C⁴²⁶**, with the three-point
 collision transported exactly through every elementary step
 (`druzkowski/REDUCTION.md` + `construct_verify.py`, run green three
 independent times on this machine). The dimensions are upper bounds of the
 plain pipeline, not optima; the slack is documented for anyone racing to
 shrink them.
- **Real form**: all three collision points are real, so F|ℝ³ is a polynomial
 local diffeomorphism with *constant* Jacobian −2 that is 3-to-1 at real
 points, a stronger real-form statement than Pinchuk's counterexample to
 the real Jacobian conjecture (nonconstant nonvanishing Jacobian, n = 2;
 the comparison crosses dimensions) (Pinchuk, Math. Z. 217 (1994)). (Pinchuk-type maps generate degree-6 field
 extensions; this map's extension has degree 3.)
- **A circulating error, preempted**: there is no "2004 Shestakov
 counterexample to the JC". Shestakov–Umirbaev (2004) proved *wildness of the
 Nagata automorphism*, a theorem about the structure of the automorphism
 group of C³, unrelated to the truth of the Jacobian Conjecture.

**What remains of the conjecture.** A "repaired conjecture" circulating on X
(constant Jacobian + no sheet loss at infinity ⇒ automorphism) is not a
conjecture but a classical **theorem**, literally equivalence (e′)⇔(a) of
(Bass–Connell–Wright, Bull. AMS 7 (1982), Thm 2.1), which lists
invertibility, birationality, injectivity, properness, and Galois-ness as
all equivalent for Keller maps in characteristic 0, any dimension. The
four-line topological proof: a Keller map is a local biholomorphism
(constant det J ≠ 0); proper + local homeomorphism gives a covering map; the
target C^n is simply connected and the *total space* C^n is connected, so
the covering has degree 1 and F is a homeomorphism; and a bijective Keller
map has polynomial inverse (Białynicki-Birula–Rosenlicht, Proc. AMS
13 (1962) 200–203; affine generalization Cynk–Rusek, Ann. Polon. Math. 56
(1991)). (In algebraic phrasing: a *connected* finite étale cover of simply
connected C^n is trivial.) All of the conjecture's content
therefore lived in non-properness, and §4 is the quantitative anatomy of
exactly that: the non-properness locus is the quartic V(D), the sheets lost
over it are the walled roots of the covering cubic, and the fully-lost locus
Γ is where all three merge and escape. The conjecture didn't fail
mysteriously; it failed by the one mechanism the classical theorem never
covered, in its minimal possible form.

## 8. A second counterexample, and a pattern

Within a day of the announcement, a second counterexample appeared in the
announcement replies, found by a *different model family* (GPT-5.6 Sol,
prompted by @hari65535) and sitting there nearly unnoticed. G : C⁴ → C⁴:

```
G₁ = −(1+xy)²z − y³(1+xy)
G₂ = 2x(1+xy)z + (1+xy)³w + y²(7 + 12xy + 4x²y²)
G₃ = 2x²z + 3x(1+xy)²w + 2y(1 + 10xy + 6x²y²)
G₄ = 2x − 4x²y − x³w
```

det JG ≡ 4, and the four distinct points (−2,0,1,0),
(−1,0,1,−2), (1,−2,−7,14), (2,−1,0,3) all map to (−1,−4,8,−4)
(`verify/07_second_counterexample.py`). By direct computation (Singular,
`verify/g4_fibers.sing`): G has **generic degree 8**, and the collision fiber
is itself a *full* 8-point fiber: its x-eliminant is
5x⁸ − 104x⁴ + 95x² + 4 = (x−2)(x−1)(x+1)(x+2)·(5x⁴ + 25x² + 1), so the four
announced points are exactly the rational half; the other four preimages
have x-coordinates the roots of the irreducible quartic 5x⁴ + 25x² + 1.

The mechanism is visibly the same: powers of u = 1+xy, and a final
coordinate 2x − 4x²y − x³w mirroring the first map's 2x − 3x²y − x³z. And
the deep structure transfers: G is C\*-equivariant with source
weights (1,−1,−3,−2) → target (−3,−2,−1,1), the same reversed-weight
pattern. Both known counterexamples carry the reversed-weight
C\*-symmetry, but the second is not an independent draw: it was produced
by prompting with the first map as the example, and the u = 1+xy mechanism
is visibly transplanted. What this shows is that the equivariant structure
travels with the mechanism, not that every counterexample carries one; the
ansatz of §9 remains a conjecture, now with two instances of a single
mechanism. (We verify G's basic certificates here and leave its full
anatomy, covering structure, monodromy, image, as the first exercise of
the program §9 proposes.)

## 9. New questions this map opens

1. **Minimal degree in dimension 3.** This map has degree 7; degree 2 is
 impossible (Wang, J. Algebra 65 (1980)). Do counterexamples of
 degree 3–6 exist? The terrain is open: even the general
 degree-3 case of JC₃ was never proven: known results cover only special
 families (homogeneous maps in dimension 3, the strongest, by de Bondt–van
 den Essen 2005 cited above; gradient maps in dimension ≤ 3; symmetric
 cubic-linear maps).
2. **Minimal generic degree: exactly 3, settled by classical results.**
 Degree 2 is impossible in every dimension: a degree-2 extension is Galois,
 and a Galois Keller map is an automorphism, (g)⇒(a) of (BCW,
 Bull. AMS 7 (1982), Thm 2.1; due to Campbell, Math. Ann. 205 (1973)
 243–248; algebraic proofs Razar, Wright, Oda). So this map achieves the
 minimal possible generic degree. **v1.1:**
 in dimension 3, *every* generic degree n ≥ 3 occurs: the seed-lift
 family of jacobianfun.org (A. Gallagher + GPT-5.6-Sol) achieves each n,
 and the review re-derived their uniform argument in full and built the
 n = 7 instance symbolically as a fresh check
 (`audit/jacobianfun_n7_check.py`: polynomial lift, det ≡ 1, seven
 simple roots; the construction is theirs, the verification ours).
 (Generic degrees 3 and 8 (§8) both
 occur. Contrast with the plane, where published results force both
 degrees of any counterexample to be composite (Magnus; Nakai–Baba), and a
 recent unrefereed preprint claims every *prime* extension degree
 impossible (arXiv 2407.13795, preprint): prime generic degree 3 is
 a genuinely higher-dimensional phenomenon.)
3. **Which monodromy groups and missed loci occur?** Here: S₃, and a single
 C\*-orbit. Is the missed locus always uniruled of codimension ≥ 2?
4. **The equivariant ansatz.** Does every (minimal) counterexample carry a
 quasi-homogeneous C\*-symmetry? Two instances now exist, one mechanism,
 across two dimensions and model families (§8), but they are not
 independent draws. The weight structure here collapses
 the whole anatomy to one cubic family: enumerate weight systems and
 search degree 3–6 within them.
